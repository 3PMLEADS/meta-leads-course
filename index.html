Everyone is bidding. Your daily budget is your bid.

Every time someone opens Facebook, there's a silent auction. Every business wants to show their ad. The more you spend per day, the more often Meta shows your ad. Think of it like a TV slot — more budget, more airtime.

Good creative + good copy = cheaper leads.

Budget gets you in the auction. But your ad wins the auction. A great ad with a lower budget beats a boring ad with a big budget — every time.

Section 3 of 8 — Builds on section 2

The 3 big mistakes

Most people waste money on Meta ads. It's almost always one of these three reasons.

Mistake 01 — The offer

Bad offer — be specific or be ignored

"Contact us" is not an offer. Nobody stops scrolling for that. Your offer needs to be so good they feel silly saying no. Free quote. Free consult. Same-day response. First job discount. Something real, something specific.

Don't worry if you lose a little on the first job. A happy customer comes back — and they bring their friends. You'll make it back 10x over time.

Mistake 02 — The timeline

Emotional decisions — this one kills more campaigns than anything

Let new ads run for 7 days. Full stop. No touching. No panicking. No turning it off at day 3 because it "isn't working." Premature emotional decisions are the number one reason ads fail.

The algorithm needs time to search through 500,000+ people and find who's most likely to fill in your form. Give it the runway to do so. 7 days. Not 2.

Mistake 03 — The creative

Terrible creative — the numbers don't lie

CTR < 1%
Click-through rate is too low
Fix the creative ✗
CPC > $0.50
Cost per click is too high
Fix the creative ✗

If either of these numbers are off — don't touch the audience, don't change the budget. Fix the ad.

These three mistakes account for 90% of wasted ad spend. Avoid them and you're already ahead of most.

Section 4 of 8 — Builds on section 3

Your audience

The secret no agency will tell you

Your creative IS your targeting.

Meta reads your ad — the image, the words, the offer — and figures out who to show it to. You don't need to overthink the audience. The right creative finds the right people automatically. I know — cool, right?

What to actually set

Location first. Everything else is optional.

📍 Location (must do)

City, suburb or radius. Only where you actually work. Set this and you're basically done.

Everything else (later)

Age, interests, behaviours. Only add these once you're scaling budget heavily. Not needed to start.

Also add exclusions — exclude other states, areas you don't service. Meta sometimes shows ads to people who visited your target area but don't live there. Cut those out.

Your total addressable market must be over 500,000 people. Too small and Meta can't find who converts.

The awareness funnel

Start at the bottom. Scale up.

Think of your audience in three levels. Always start at the bottom where the money is. Move higher up the funnel as your budget grows.

Top of funnel — scale here last

Cold — Never heard of you

Broad interest targeting. Cheap to reach, harder to convert. Only once lower funnel is profitable.

▼

Middle of funnel — scale here next

Aware but not ready yet

They know they have a problem. They're comparing. Use different creative and copy for this group. Never use the same ad as bottom of funnel.

▼

Bottom of funnel — START HERE

Warm prospects

Past customers, website visitors, page engagers, past enquiries. Ready to buy. Cheapest to convert. Always start here.

$ $$ $ Paying customers

When your cost per lead rises or your creative stops working — don't panic. Move up the funnel. New audience level, fresh creative, new copy. That's how you scale.

The 1-second rule

You have one second to stop someone mid-scroll.

Your creative does all the heavy lifting. Not your targeting. Not your budget. The ad itself. Three things need to happen the moment someone sees your ad.

Copy structure — 3 parts

Hook. Offer. CTA. That's all you need.

Part 01 — Hook

Call out your customer directly

"Melbourne homeowners — tired of tradies who don't show up?" Make it feel personal. Talk to one person, not a crowd.

Part 02 — Offer

Be specific. Not vague.

"Free quote in 24 hours — no obligation." Not "contact us for more info." Specific always wins.

Part 03 — CTA

Tell them exactly what to do

"Click Get Quote below." Simple. Direct. Don't be clever here — be clear.

Image vs video

Both work. Start with images if you don't have video.

Real photos of your actual work always beat stock images. Once you're getting leads, shoot a quick 30-second video. Hook them in the first 3 seconds or they're gone.

Testing framework — keep variables isolated

Test one thing at a time. Change two things and you'll never know what worked.

Phase 1
Creative testing
Test 3–5 different images or videos. Same headline. Same copy. Same CTA. Let data pick the winner. Run for 7 days.
Phase 2
Headline testing
Take the winning creative. Test 2–3 different headlines. Keep everything else the same. 7 days.
Phase 3
CTA testing
Winning creative + winning headline. Now test 2–3 different CTAs. "Get free quote" vs "Book now" vs "Claim your spot." 7 days.
Phase 4
Primary text testing
Test 2–3 versions of your ad body copy. Then let the winner run. Rinse and repeat every month.

Never change the image and the headline at the same time. You won't know which change made the difference. Isolate. Test. Decide.

Section 8 of 12 — Your ongoing plan

Your 4-week plan

Follow this every month. Rinse and repeat. This is how you build a lead machine that gets cheaper over time.

Week 1
Launch + creative test
Go live with 3–5 different creatives (images or videos). Same headline. Same copy. Same CTA. Let Meta serve them. Do not touch anything. Check back in 7 days.
Week 2
Headline test
Kill the worst performers. Keep the top 1–2 creatives. Now test 2–3 different headlines. Keep everything else exactly the same. Let data decide. 7 days.

Week 4

Test 2–3 versions of your body copy. Pick the winner. Let the full ad run. Start scaling budget by +20% every 3–4 days. Then start again next month.

Good hook Call out the frustration

Tired of cleaners who cancel last minute? We show up. Every time. Guaranteed. Get a free quote today — Melbourne bookings available this week.

Why it works: Opens by calling out a real pain point. Answers it immediately with a bold claim. Specific location. Creates urgency with "this week." No waffle.

Why it works: Social proof upfront (127 customers). Removes price anxiety early. Three fast bullets that hit: free, no commitment, fast. Clear next step. Easy to act on.

Offers — what you're giving them

Strong offer vs weak offer.

Weak offer Vague and forgettable
▼
Get in touch for more information about our services.

Why it fails: There's no offer here. "More information" is not a reward. It asks for effort and gives nothing in return. Nobody fills in a form for this.

Strong offer Specific and irresistible
▼
Get a free, no-obligation quote within 24 hours. First-time customers get 10% off their first job. We call you — you don't have to chase us.

Why it works: Three specific things they get. Removes risk ("no obligation"). Speed commitment ("24 hours"). Bonus incentive. Removes effort ("we call you"). Hard to say no to.

CTAs — the close

Tell them exactly what to do. Be boring. Be direct.

Weak CTA Vague and passive
▼
Feel free to reach out if you're interested!

Why it fails: "Feel free" sounds optional. "If you're interested" plants doubt. No instruction. No urgency. No reason to act now.

Strong CTA Direct and action-driven
▼
Tap "Get Free Quote" below. Takes 30 seconds. We'll call you back today.

Why it works: Tells them the exact button to tap. Removes friction ("30 seconds"). Time commitment from you ("today"). Fast. Clear. No confusion.

A great offer is the difference between a full inbox and a silent one. Use this builder to construct an offer people can't ignore.

The offer formula

4 parts. Every strong offer has all of them.

1. What they get

The specific thing you're giving them. Free quote, free session, free inspection, discount, report.

2. The timeframe

How fast do they get it? Within 2 hours, same day, within 24 hours. Faster = more compelling.

3. Remove the risk

No obligation. No lock-in. No credit card. Cancel anytime. Lower the barrier to say yes.

4. The bonus (optional)

Stack something extra on top. First-time discount. Free checklist. Priority booking. Makes it feel like more value.

Section 12 of 12 — Troubleshooting

When things go wrong

Something's not working. Here's how to diagnose it and fix it. Click each problem to see the solution.

Creative problems

Your ad isn't getting clicks or engagement

!
My CTR is below 1% — nobody is clicking
▼

Your creative isn't stopping the scroll. The image or first line of copy isn't compelling enough. Here's what to do:

1
Don't change your audience or budget. The problem is the ad itself.
2
Test a completely new hook — the first line of your copy. Try calling out a pain point more directly.
3
Try a different image — a real photo of your work, a before/after, or a short video. Real beats stock every time.
4
Run the new creative for 7 days before judging. Give it time.
!
My CPC is above $0.50 — clicks are too expensive
▼

High CPC means Meta is struggling to find people who want to click. The creative isn't resonating enough with the audience.

1
Check your hook — is it speaking to a real, specific problem your customer has?
2
Check your image — is it eye-catching? Is it relevant? Is it professional?
3
Make sure your offer is clear in the first 3 lines of copy — don't make them read to find out what they get.
4
Do NOT expand your audience to try and fix this. Fix the creative first.
?
Meta keeps rejecting my ad — what do I do?
▼

Meta has strict policies. Most rejections come from a handful of common issues.

1
Check the rejection reason in Ads Manager — Meta usually tells you why.
2
Avoid before/after images that imply dramatic transformation — Meta flags these especially in health and beauty.
3
Don't use personal attributes in your targeting copy — e.g. "Are you a diabetic?" Meta will reject this.
4
Remove any guarantees like "100% results guaranteed" — Meta doesn't allow absolute claims.
5
If rejected unfairly, click "Request Review" in Ads Manager. A human will look at it. Often gets approved on review.

Lead quality problems

Leads are coming in but they're not converting

!
I'm getting leads but nobody answers the phone
▼

Speed is everything. The faster you call, the higher your contact rate. After 1 hour, conversion drops dramatically.

1
Set up instant lead notifications — connect your lead form to email or a CRM so you're alerted the moment a lead comes in.
2
Call within the hour. Ideally within 15 minutes. The lead filled in the form while thinking about you — that window closes fast.
3
If no answer, send an SMS immediately: "Hi [name], it's [your name] from [business]. I saw your enquiry — happy to chat when suits! Feel free to reply here."
4
Follow up again at 24 hours and 48 hours. Most sales happen on the 2nd or 3rd contact.
!
My leads are cheap but terrible quality — wrong people
▼

Low quality leads usually mean your form isn't filtering properly, or your creative is attracting the wrong person.

1
Add a qualifying question to your form — "What suburb are you in?" or "When are you looking to start?" This filters out browsers.
2
Switch your form from "More Volume" to "Higher Intent" — adds a review screen which filters out accidental taps.
3
Check your ad copy — if it's too vague, it attracts everyone. Make your copy specific about who you're for and who you're not.
4
Add exclusions to your audience — exclude suburbs or areas you can't service.

Scaling problems

Things were working — now they're not

?
My cost per lead keeps rising and I don't know why
▼

This is normal. It happens to every campaign over time. Your creative has fatigued — the same audience has seen it too many times.

1
Check your frequency — if it's above 3–4, your audience has seen the ad too many times. Time to refresh.
2
Launch a new creative — new image or video, new hook. Keep the offer the same if it was working.
3
Move up the funnel — try targeting middle-of-funnel audiences with a fresh creative suited to a colder audience.
4
This is the monthly cycle. Expect to refresh creatives every 4–6 weeks.
i
I increased my budget and now my CPL went up — why?
▼

Scaling budget too fast resets the learning phase. Meta has to relearn who to show your ad to, which costs more while it figures it out.

1
Never increase budget by more than 20% every 3–4 days. Slow and steady wins here.
2
If you jumped budget too fast, drop it back to the previous level and wait 7 days for performance to stabilise.
3
For big budget jumps, consider duplicating the ad set instead of increasing the existing one.
